DC Lacrosse

Looking to Play Lacrosse in the DC,VA,MD area?

Welcome to the DC Lacrosse site! Here you'll find information on leagues, tournamants, and organizations for you to play lacrosse, get involved and help grow the game.


Up until winter 2014 there was a meetup.com group for pick-up lacrosse in Washington, DC. Thanks to meetup.com's fancy baked-in search optimization, the group's page would always appear in the top of a Google search for lacrosse in DC. It served as a de facto information gateway on the various lacrosse teams and leagues in the DC metro area.


Since the Meetup group's disbandment, this site aims to serve as your online clearinghouse for information about adult lacrosse in the DC, VA, MD area.

Women's Leagues

Women's Leagues

First Ladies Lax is the only DC-based team out there for post-collegiate play. You can find them via their Meetup group or visit them on Facebook. They play in the Women's Mid-Atlantic Post-Collegiate Lacrosse League. The league is comprised of teams located throughout the Baltimore, Annapolis, DC, and Northern Virginia areas. The spring season runs March through May on Sundays.


While you're looking to connect with other women laxers, help promote girls' lacrosse in the DC area. Donate to Winners Lacrosse and Grow the Game!

Men's Leagues

Men's Leagues

Basically there are two categories available: Open League or Master's League. Open League is for players with a minimum high school level experience. Most players have either college club or NCAA collegiate team experience. The players are also mostly post-college age, though you may have some high school or current college age players on your team.

Players over the age of 35 are eligible play in the Master's League. Experience ranges but most players have past (way past) experience at the college level. But hey, better to be a has-been than a never-was!

What type of league are you looking for?

Open League

Are you a current high school or collegiate player looking for offseason playing time to sharpen your skills? Or are you a post-collegiate player looking for an opportunity to keep playing after college at a high level? If so check out the Stars and Stripes Lacrosse League. Games are held at area high schools in Northern Virginia, Montgomery County, and on the campus of Georgetown University.


If you live near Fredericksburg VA, there is a new outdoor league at the Fredericksburg Field House starting in summer 2015.


In the Baltimore suburbs, Howard County Recreation and Parks offers a Open Men's Division in the fall.


If you're looking for indoor ("box") lacrosse action, check out Performance Lacrosse. They offer a competitive adult league in the fall and winter seasons at the Wheaton Regional Park in Maryland.

Masters League

The Maryland Masters Lacrosse League is the best outlet for guys over 35 years of age who can't quit playing lacrosse. As the league's slogan says, they play "smarter not harder." MMLL has an organized spring field league that plays at various locations in Howard, Anne Arundel, and Baltimore Counties.


If you're looking for some pre-spring games to rev up for the field game, check out the league at API in Gambrills, MD. It's a modifield field rules version of the game played in a heated, indoor turf field. The field is slightly shorter than a regulation outdoor field so teams play one fewer attack and close defenseman, i.e. 7v7 with a goalie.


In the summer and fall seasons, Howard County Recreation & Parks offers masters level field lacrosse leagues.


Red Line Athletic Club offers masters level indoor lacrosse in the fall (through mid-December) and in the early spring. Red Line games are held at the Wheaton Regional Park's indoor turf facility.

Tournaments

Looking for Tournaments?


While the weather's warm, you can find a number of tournaments in the area. Around June, on the campus of Mt. Saint Mary's College in Emmittsburg MD (10 miles from Frederick), there's the Blue and Gray Lax Battle. It's an open division tournament consisting of post-college alumni and select travel teams.


In mid-June, there's a great opportunity to support our troops by participating in a 24 hour non-stop lacrosse game - Shootout for Soldiers!


In August, there's the Ocean City Lacrosse Classic. The tournament offers women's, open division, masters, and even grand masters (age +45).


Finally in early October, there's Laxtoberfest at Anne Arundel Community College. Just like the Ocean City Lacrosse Classic, it also offers tourament play for all divisions.

Grow the Game

Help Grow the Game!

You've enjoyed playing lacrosse and gained lifelong memories and friendships from it. Help spread the sport, donate to Winners Lacrosse.


Winners Lacrosse is a 501(c)3 nonprofit organization that was started in October 2000 to provide Washington, DC boys and girls, from historically underserved neighborhoods, the opportunity to learn the game of lacrosse and the life skills associated with playing a team sport. Winners Lacrosse uses the game as the vehicle to instill values, education and life sustaining skills that will give young boys and girls the tools to be successful on and off the field. Winners Lacrosse clinics, camps, teams and after school programs include these virtues as part of the curriculum.
